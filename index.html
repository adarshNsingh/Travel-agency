About Us

Bihar Tourism is the initiative of the Ministry of Tourism, Bihar Government to promote and incentivise the tourism opportunities in the state. The fundamental activities of the department include developing the conditions and infrastructure to make the state conducive to travel and tourism. In addition, Bihar Tourism is also focused on helping tourists find destinations, routes and experiences that are to their liking. Whether it is in the form of circuits that cover a specific set of spiritual, natural, or historical places, or different types of food and festivals, Bihar Tourism offers something for everyone. From helping tourists plan and book their travel and stay to ensuring they experience the beauty and culture of Bihar without missing anything from their itinerary, Bihar Tourism is a friend for all tourists of the state. Arrange your transportation and stays conveniently at every stop of your travel route with Bihar Tourism. Get relevant and reliable information about your destinations

Popular Destinations

Glass Bridge

The Rajgir Glass Bridge is a thrilling, high-altitude glass skywalk offering breathtaking valley views and a heart-pounding experience. Ideally visited during cooler months, with online booking being essential due to limited access and popularity. A must-do for adventure seekers visiting Rajgir’s Nature Safari.

Iskcon Temple

The Patna ISKCON temple is a majestic, spiritually rich landmark offering devotional rituals, cultural events, comfortable amenities, and architectural beauty—all with free entry. Let me know if you'd like help planning a visit or booking their guest house!

Shersha Suri Tomb

Sher Shah Suri’s Tomb stands as a majestic architectural masterpiece—a serene island of history, craftsmanship, and cultural legacy. If you're exploring Sasaram, it’s a must-visit. Want tips on guides, nearby stays, or travel routes? Just say the word!

Garib Nath Temple

Baba Garibnath Temple is a historic and deeply revered Shiva shrine in central Muzaffarpur. With its rich folklore, lively festivals, free entry, and inclusion in Bihar’s spiritual fabric, it offers an immersive devotional experience.

Maa Janki Mandir

Punaura Dham, located near Sitamarhi in Bihar, is a revered Hindu pilgrimage site believed to be the birthplace of Goddess Sita (Maa Janaki).With support from the Bihar government, Punaura Dham is undergoing major redevelopment to enhance facilities and infrastructure, aiming to make it a prominent stop in the Ramayana tourism circuit

Rajendra Prasad House

Dr Rajendra Prasad’s ancestral home in Ziradei is a cherished but underdeveloped heritage site. It holds deep historical and emotional value, especially during annual memorial events, though it requires better visitor facilities and infrastructure upgrades to become a fully welcomed destination for guests and students of history..

Tutla Bhawani

Nestled in the lush valley of the Kaimur (Rohtas) Hills, Tutla Bhawani Waterfall plunges from approximately 180 ft (55 m) between two towering cliffs, creating a striking natural spectacle  . Flowing into the serene Kachhuaar River, it forms a beautiful emerald pool at the base amid thick greenery  A small Maa Tutla Bhawani temple, along with an idol of Goddess Jagaddhatri Durga, sits on a rocky ledge beside the falls, making it both a scenic and spiritual destination  . Historical inscriptions date back to 1158 AD, adding a layer of cultural depth to the locale  .

Shanti Stupa

The Vishwa Shanti Stupa, also known as the World Peace Pagoda, is a stunning white marble stupa perched atop Ratnagiri Hill (Gridhakūṭa) in Rajgir, Bihar, standing at an elevation of about 400 m. It was first built in 1969 by the Japanese Buddhist monk Nichidatsu Fujii as a symbol of peace and non‑violence; the current structure was completed following renovations in the early 1990s

Pawapuri Jain Temple

The Jal Mandir in Pawapuri, Bihar, is a revered Jain pilgrimage site marking the cremation spot of Lord Mahavira, the 24th Tirthankara, who attained Nirvana in 527 BC. Built by Royal Kin: Constructed by King Nandivardhan, Mahavira's elder brother, the temple is nestled in a lotus-filled pond that formed when devotees removed soil after his cremation. Sacred Footprints: At the heart of the temple lies the Charan Paduka, the sacred footprint of Mahavira, enshrined for worship
